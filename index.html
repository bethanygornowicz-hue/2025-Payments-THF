Key insights & 2026 action plan

  • Best month October led at $41,912 — nearly $12K above your two weakest months. Billing spiked to $30,716 that month (vs. ~$22K average). Identify what drove it (fall re-enrollment cycle, rate changes, new members) and replicate in 2026.
  • February February ($29,600) was your softest month. Post-New Year dropout is common — a mid-January re-engagement campaign, check-in call blitz, or "stay on track" promo could recover $3,000–$5,000 here. Prioritize this in your 2026 calendar.
  • November November ($29,573) matched February as the weakest. Billing dropped to $20,240 — the lowest all year. A Black Friday membership promo or holiday gift card push could meaningfully lift this month in 2026.
  • Oct refunds Despite being your best revenue month, October also had the highest refund total at $1,333. Net revenue was still strong, but it's worth auditing what drove those reversals to prevent recurrence.
  • Revenue mix Auto-billing accounts for 80% of revenue ($323K) — a healthy, predictable base. Membership direct payments add 22% ($87K). Every new recurring member you add is worth approximately $371/year at the average billing rate.
  • Membership types Import Memberships ($87.5K) and Gym Memberships ($77.5K) are your top two billing categories. Group Training ($49K) and Personal Training ($46K) are nearly tied — a focused PT growth push could make PT your #3 revenue driver by year end.
  • Product sales General products ($10,909) and Drop-In fees ($5,517) lead your product revenue. Supplements ($4,751) are strong — a dedicated display or checkout upsell push could cross $6K in 2026. Drinks ($2,987) and Apparel ($959) have room to grow with better placement.
  • Payment mix Check ($33.6K) and Cash ($28K) together account for 15% of revenue — meaningful manual payment volume. If even 20% of those convert to auto-billing, you add ~$12K in predictable recurring revenue with less admin overhead.
  • 2026 forecast Linear trend projects $442,569 — an +8% increase (+$33K) over 2025. This is a conservative baseline. Closing the February and November gaps alone adds ~$15K. A PT membership growth push could add another $10–$20K. Realistic upside: $460–$475K.
